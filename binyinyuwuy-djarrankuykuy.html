Binyinyuwuy Djarrankuykuy

Dick Binyinyuwuy Djarrankuykuy (c. 1928–1982) was a leading Aboriginal artist from the island of Milingimbi off the coast of the Northern Territory of Australia. He belonged to the Djarrankuykuy clan of the Djambarrpuyngu people. During World War II he was among the group of Aboriginal men enlisted by Squadron Leader Donald Thomson for the Northern Territory Special Reconnaissance Unit to protect Australia's northern shoreline from Japanese invasion. He became an artist after the war, in the 1950s.





== Biography ==

Binyinyuwuy was born in 1928 in Ramingining, in central Arnhem Land, Australia. He belongs to the Djambarrpuyngu language group of the Dhuwa moiety. Djambarrpuyngu is both a language group and a clan, with most of its members living in Milingimbi, like Binyinyuwuy and Galiwin’ku. As a young man, he moved to the island of Milingimbi, just off the northern coast of Arnhem Land. The town of Milingimbi was established by Reverend James Watson from the Methodist Overseas Mission in 1916, and later re-established in 1951 after the residents were evacuated during World War II. The many missionaries at the settlement, like TT Webb and Edgar Wells, promoted and encouraged the people to continue making art.
