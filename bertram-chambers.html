Bertram Chambers

Admiral Bertram Mordaunt Chambers, CB (3 October 1866 – 27 April 1945) was a Royal Navy officer who served in Australia and Canada.





== Life and career ==





=== Early life and career ===

Born in London, Chambers was the son of barrister Charles Harcourt Chambers and Lucibella Chambers, née Hare. His paternal grandfather was Sir Charles Harcourt Chambers, puisne judge of the Supreme Court of Bombay. Chambers was educated at Clifton College and Stubbington House School, before entering HMS Britannia as a cadet in 1879, placing twenty-seventh out of thirty-seven successful candidates. Passing out of Britannia in 1881, Chambers was appointed to HMS Monarch in which, as a midshipman, he was present at the bombardment of Alexandria in 1882. In 1883 he was appointed to HMS Satellite, remaining in her until 1886.

Promoted to lieutenant in 1889, Chambers was awarded the Shadwell Testimonial for naval surveying in 1894 and 1895. He was promoted to the rank of commander in 1900 and captain in 1905. He then attended a signals course and a war course in Portsmouth, finishing in 1906. He became flag captain of the battleship HMS Bulwark in 1907, the flagship of Rear-Admiral Frank Finnis of the Nore Division, Home Fleet.
