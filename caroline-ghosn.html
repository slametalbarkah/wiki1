Caroline Ghosn

Caroline Ghosn (Arabic: كارولين غصن; born January 29, 1987) is an American businesswoman. She is the eldest child of former Nissan and Renault CEO, Carlos Ghosn. In 2011, she founded Levo (formerly Levo League), a professional network dedicated to helping millennials navigate the workplace, and has been managing its development since then.

Ghosn is an active member of the World Economic Forum’s Global Shapers community and attended and spoke at the annual World Economic Forum in Davos in 2014.



Ghosn was presented as a debutante at the Bal des débutantes in Paris in 2006.





== Education ==

Ghosn graduated from Stanford University in 2008 with a bachelor’s degree. She has lived in six countries and speaks four languages.





== Business career ==



In 2011 Ghosn founded Levo to help millennials achieve success in the workplace.

Ghosn currently serves as the CEO and Chairman of Levo's Board of Directors. Levo has an audience of over 9 million members globally, and 30 local Levo chapters around the world.
